## Benchmark comparisons (Fortune 500 COOs)

**Tim Cook (Apple COO → CEO)**
- BS Industrial Engineering — Auburn University (non-target)
- MBA — Duke Fuqua
- Career: IBM ops → Compaq → Apple ops director → COO → CEO
- Key lesson: Non-target undergrad + non-HBS MBA + exceptional ops execution = COO at the world's most valuable company

**Mary Barra (GM COO → CEO)**
- BS Electrical Engineering — Kettering University (GMI co-op)
- MBA — Stanford GSB
- Career: Entire career at GM; plant manager → VP Global HR → VP Global Product Development → COO → CEO
- Key lesson: Internal climb, 30+ years, deep plant-floor credibility from day 1

**Ron Vachris (Costco COO → CEO)**
- No MBA
- Career: Forklift driver → warehouse clerk → assistant GM → GM → regional SVP → COO → CEO
- Key lesson: Results at scale + internal visibility + P&L ownership matters more than credentials at some companies

**Pattern across all three:** Engineering or technical background → early ops credibility → P&L ownership → internal promotion → COO. MBA is not universal but dramatically accelerates timeline for external moves.

## Wealth mechanism — how COO becomes the money

| Mechanism | When | How |
|---|---|---|
| Executive comp (base + bonus) | VP Ops onward | $300K–$800K+ at Fortune 500 VP level |
| LTI / RSUs | Director onward | Long-term incentives compound significantly over 10+ years |
| COO total comp | Fortune 500 COO | $2M–$10M+ (base + bonus + equity) |
| PE Operating Partner | Post-COO pivot | Carried interest = wealth multiplier; 20% of fund profits on $500M+ fund = $100M+ over fund life |
| Industrial PE Partner | Endgame | Running portfolio company operations; carried interest as primary wealth mechanism |

**The PE pivot window opens at COO.** Operating Partners at PE firms are almost exclusively recruited from COO, President, or CEO backgrounds. The Fortune 500 COO role is not just a career goal — it's the credential that unlocks the PE carried interest track.
