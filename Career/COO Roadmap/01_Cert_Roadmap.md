# 01 — Certification Roadmap

**Canonical control:** [[Career/COO Roadmap/00_Project_Control]]

> Sourced from COO path research session, June 2026. Based on Fortune 500 C-suite data, ASQ salary surveys, Spencer Stuart 2024 C-Suite Snapshot, and analysis of lean-culture Fortune 500 companies (GE, Honeywell, Danaher, Eaton, Parker Hannifin).

---

## Priority stack — in order

| # | Certification | Urgency | Timing | Why |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | LSS Green Belt | 🔴 Do now | Before Aug 2026 | Table stakes at every Fortune 500 ops role. GE made it minimum for promotion companywide. Yellow Belt is already behind the curve. |
| 2 | LSS Black Belt — ASQ CSSBB | 🔴 Must have | Age 24–25 (2–3 yrs post-grad) | COOs at lean-culture industrials (GE, Honeywell, Eaton, Danaher, Parker) almost universally hold BB or higher. ASQ version requires documented project ROI — Mersen $500K+ project already qualifies. |
| 3 | APICS CPIM | 🟡 Strong signal | Age 25–27 (3–5 yrs post-grad) | Gold standard for mfg ops and supply chain. CPIM-certified professionals earn 17–25% more (ASCM 2022). Covers MRP, S&OP, demand planning, shop floor execution — exactly what a COO needs to speak fluently. |
| 4 | MBA — top-10 | 🟡 Strategic | Age 26–29 (enroll 2030–2032) | ~41–46% of Fortune 500 C-suite hold MBAs. For industrial ops COO track, Kellogg/Ross/Booth outperform HBS in Midwest placement. GMAT 720+ target. See [[Career/COO Roadmap/02_MBA_Roadmap]]. |
| 5 | PMP | 🟢 Nice to have | Age 26–28 (4–6 yrs post-grad) | +22% salary premium per PMI data. Requires 36 months documented PM experience. Complementary to LSS, not competing. Worth getting after Black Belt. |
| 6 | APICS CSCP | 🟢 Nice to have | Age 30–35 | More strategic than CPIM — covers global supply chain, sourcing, logistics. 25% salary premium, 65% stronger hiring potential. Choose one of CPIM or CSCP early; get both by mid-career. |
| 7 | LSS Master Black Belt | 🟢 Nice to have | Age 35–40 | Enterprise deployment level. Relevant if heading toward VP Operational Excellence. Avg salary $163K–$200K+. Common among COOs at GE/Honeywell/Danaher lineage companies. |
| 8 | Harvard AMP / MIT AMP | ⬜ Late career | Age 44–50 | 42-person cohort, $65–73K, 5 weeks, requires 15–20 yrs experience. Employer-nominated and sponsored. A milestone that signals you've arrived — not a credential you pursue. |

## Certifications to skip

| Cert | Why skip |
|---|---|
| CPA / CFA | CFO-track, not COO-track. Get financial literacy through MBA and on-the-job exposure instead. |
| ISO 9001 / ISO Lead Auditor | Quality management roles, not COO track. Useful to understand conceptually — not worth certifying. |
| OSHA 30 | EHS-track credential. Not a COO differentiator. |

## LSS Green Belt — action plan

**What:** Lean Six Sigma Green Belt via SSGI (Sam already holds Yellow Belt from them) or ASQ

**Why now:** Highest-leverage near-term credential. Goes on resume before Summer 2027 applications. Required at most Fortune 500 rotational programs. Oakland University also offers LSS Green Belt coursework — check if it counts toward degree requirements.

**Cost:** SSGI Green Belt ~$200–400 online. ASQ CSSGB exam ~$438 (member) or $538 (non-member).

**Time:** 2–4 weeks of study on top of existing Yellow Belt knowledge.

**Target completion:** Before Aug 15, 2026 (application season opens).

## ASQ CSSBB — why it's different from other Black Belt certs

The ASQ Certified Six Sigma Black Belt is the most credible version because it requires:
- Documented proof of completed Six Sigma project(s)
- Demonstrated financial impact / ROI from those projects
- 3 years of work experience in one or more areas of the CSSBB Body of Knowledge

Sam's Mersen grinding-to-sanding project ($500K+ verified annual cost savings, 185 part numbers) is exactly the kind of ASQ-qualifying project most people spend years trying to find after getting their Green Belt. This gives Sam a head start of 2–3 years on the typical Black Belt timeline.

**Next step:** Review ASQ CSSBB Body of Knowledge and confirm project documentation format required.

## APICS CPIM — what it covers

Certified in Production and Inventory Management focuses on:
- Demand management and forecasting
- Master planning (MRP, MPS)
- Shop floor execution and scheduling
- Inventory management and optimization
- Continuous improvement and quality
- S&OP processes
- Technology in operations

**Why it matters for COO track:** COOs in manufacturing/industrial need to speak fluently in all of the above. CPIM is the standard credential that proves you can. Recruiters at Eaton, Parker Hannifin, Whirlpool, and Caterpillar look for it on VP/Director-level ops candidates.
